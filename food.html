FOOD

Recommendations: Nasi Lemak | Sarawak Laksa | Penang Char Kueh Tiao | Kolo Mee




NASI LEMAK

About 'Nasi Lemak'


Fluffy aromatic rice cooked with coconut milk, pandan leaves and herbs.

Always accompanied with sambal (spicy sauce with any protein source of your likings), spiced fried chicken, hard boiled eggs,

slices of cucumber and some fried anchovies and peanuts.

Five Malaysian chefs reveal their go-to spots for nasi lemak

SARAWAK LAKSA

About: Sarawak Laksa


One of the signature dishes in Kuching, Sarawak.

It's unique compared to the other laksa dishes in the country.

The broth has the perfect blend of spiciness, herbs and tanginess blended, with the light creamy taste of coconut milk.

In 2015, the late American celebrity chef Anthony Bourdain described the dish as: 'Breakfast of the Gods'.

PENANG CHAR KUEH TIAO

About 'Penang Char Kueh Tiao'


It's the food that defines Penang.

Penang Char Kueh Tiao is a simple rice noodle dish typically made with soy sauce, eggs, cockles, bean sprouts and a couple of shrimp.

Hailed by the BBC as the humble 'King of Noodles'.

Famous stalls in Penang

KOLO MEE

About 'Kolo Mee'


A dish originated from Sarawak.

Kolo Mee consists of the famous springy egg noodles coated with sauce.

Usually drenched in pork lard and shallots oil, topped with char siew (barbeque pork)

and minced pork and complemented with sides of pickled chillies and a bowl of soup.
