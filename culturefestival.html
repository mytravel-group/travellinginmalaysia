CULTURAL CELEBRATIONS



Races: Indian | Malay | Chinese

DEEPAVALI

Indians


Deepavali (Diwali) is the primary festival of the Indians in Malaysia and is also known as the festival of lights.

The entrances of Hindu homes are decorated with the ‘kolam’, an intricate floral design on the ground which signifies religious believes.

The glow of lights, whether 'vilakku' (oil lamps fashioned out of clay) or colourful electric bulbs, brighten up the abode of both rich and poor.

HARI RAYA AIDILFITRI

Malays


Hari Raya AidilFitri is one of the cultural celebrations in Malaysia, with more than half of the country being ethnic Malays.

It is an annual celebration that marks the end of the Muslim fasting month, also known as Ramadhan.

Some families will host an ‘open house’, where they will welcome neighbours and friends to their house and celebrate together.

No Hari Raya is complete without the excellent cuisine like ketupat, curry chicken, beef rendang and satays typically offered up at 'open houses'.

CHINESE NEW YEAR

Chinese


Chinese New Year （春节）is one of the most important festivals for the Chinese people.

Many activities are held during this period such as dragon and lion dances, setting off fireworks and giving out red packets.

Lion dance (舞狮) is a form of traditional dance in Chinese culture and other Asian countries which is believed to bring good luck and fortune.
